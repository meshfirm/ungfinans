Hvordan vælger man den rigtige digitale bank?

May 2nd 2016 in Digital Banking
SEbastian krumhausen

Are you feeling challenged by finding the right bank that meets your expectations? We have put together a simple guide on how to understand these new digital banks and evaluate different features that they have

What is digital banking?

A digital bank is a bank, that conducts all of its banking operations online primarily through mobile applications. Many of the new ones are so-called "digital-first banks", meaning that from the start of the bank, they only work online without any physical presence. This way, they save a lot of costs by automating processes with real-time updates such as providing customer support through app as an example. 

For decades, the customers have preferred the traditional banks like Danske Bank, Nordea, Jyske Bank, Nykredit. However, these banks do not fill the expectations of younger customers, who are accustomed to interact through mobile interfaces and expect the same usability from their banks. 

Why use digital-only banks?

These digital-only banks are mainly operated through an app. This means, that users have an easier control over their finances thanks to better visibility. Consisting automatically updated spending information, commission-free payments abroad and the ability to freeze and unfreeze the card through the app. Some digital banks offer the ability to split your bills between friends, easily change the PIN code and trade with cryptocurrencies. 

This means, that these type of digital banks are always looking for ways to stay one step ahead of their competition with innovative features and functionalities to better enable their users to manage their daily finances. This means, that by using one of the digital banks like N26, Revolut or LUNAR, you will be one of the first ones to benefit from the latest banking innovations.
